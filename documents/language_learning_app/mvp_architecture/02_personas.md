## P-01: Returning Learner (Marco)

**Demographics:** 34 years old, Italy, learning English  
**Background:** Studied English through school and university. Achieved B1 reading comprehension but never practiced speaking. Used the language intermittently over 5+ years. Now needs English for professional growth.

### Goals
- Reactivate existing passive knowledge
- Build active speaking and writing skills
- Achieve B2 certification within 8 months

### Frustrations
- Traditional apps too basic (already knows past tense)
- Advanced content assumes too much speaking confidence
- Cannot find material that matches his "forgotten intermediate" level
- Gets bored with repetitive drills

### Skill Asymmetry
- Reading: B1, Listening: A2, Speaking: A1, Writing: A2, Grammar: B1, Vocabulary: B1
- Strong passive knowledge but weak productive skills

### Motivation
- **Primary**: Career advancement (needs English for international team meetings)
- **Secondary**: Personal satisfaction of regaining lost ability

### Accessibility Needs
- Standard UI, no special accessibility requirements
- Prefers text-heavy content with audio support

### Preferred Lesson Duration
- 15-20 minutes per session, 4-5 sessions per week

### Likely Failure Points
- Overconfidence leading to advanced lesson selection beyond actual ability
- Frustration when passive knowledge doesn't translate to active production
- Inconsistent practice due to work commitments

### Retention Risks
- Boredom if content feels too easy
- Discouragement if productive skills don't improve quickly
- Competing priorities (work, family)

### Success Definition
- Can hold a 5-minute work conversation without excessive pauses
- Scores B2 on diagnostic reassessment
- Maintains 70%+ weekly practice consistency

## P-02: Beginner with Native-Language Scaffolding (Yuki)

**Demographics:** 28 years old, Japan, learning English  
**Background:** No formal English education beyond mandatory school classes (which focused on grammar-translation). Can read basic sentences with heavy dictionary use. Cannot understand spoken English at natural speed.

### Goals
- Build basic survival English for travel
- Achieve A2 level in speaking and listening
- Understand everyday conversational English

### Frustrations
- Apps that use only English for instructions are overwhelming
- Translation-based apps don't teach natural communication
- Grammar explanations in English are incomprehensible
- Needs Japanese-language scaffolding to understand concepts

### Skill Asymmetry
- Reading: A1, Listening: Pre-A1, Speaking: Pre-A1, Writing: A1, Grammar: A1, Vocabulary: A1
- All skills at beginner level with slight reading advantage

### Motivation
- **Primary**: Planned working holiday in Canada (6 months away)
- **Secondary**: Access to English-language media and culture

### Accessibility Needs
- Native language (Japanese) support for UI, instructions, and feedback
- Clear, slow audio for listening exercises
- Visual cues and images to support comprehension
- Option to view translations for all target language content

### Preferred Lesson Duration
- 10-15 minutes per session, daily practice

### Likely Failure Points
- Cognitive overload from immersion-only approach
- Giving up when unable to produce target language
- Dependence on translation preventing natural language acquisition

### Retention Risks
- Slow progress leading to demotivation
- Competing study methods (traditional classroom, other apps)
- Social anxiety about language learning

### Success Definition
- Can order food, ask for directions, and introduce herself in English
- Understands slow, clear spoken English on familiar topics
- Achieves A2 on diagnostic reassessment within 6 months

## P-03: Work-Focused Learner (Carlos)

**Demographics:** 41 years old, Mexico, learning English  
**Background:** Has functional English from self-study and media exposure. Can read work emails with moderate effort. Struggles with speaking in meetings and writing professional documents. Previously studied to A2 level but plateaued.

### Goals
- Write clear work emails without assistance
- Participate in virtual meetings with confidence
- Understand industry-specific terminology
- Achieve B1+ in professional contexts

### Frustrations
- General English apps don't cover professional scenarios
- Business English courses are expensive and time-fixed
- Cannot practice workplace-specific situations
- Needs practical, immediately usable content

### Skill Asymmetry
- Reading: A2/B1 (with dictionary), Listening: A2, Speaking: A1/A2, Writing: A2, Grammar: A2, Vocabulary: A2
- Receptive skills stronger than productive; workplace vocabulary weaker than general

### Motivation
- **Primary**: Mandated English proficiency for management track at multinational company
- **Secondary**: Reduced reliance on colleagues for translation

### Accessibility Needs
- Time-efficient interactions (no games or fluff)
- Business-context examples and vocabulary
- Professional communication templates and models
- Schedule flexibility (early mornings, late evenings)

### Preferred Lesson Duration
- 10 minute micro-lessons, 7 days per week (fits between meetings)

### Likely Failure Points
- Skipping lessons when work is busy (paradoxically, exactly when most needed)
- Focusing only on receptive skills (reading/listening) and avoiding speaking/writing
- Perfectionism preventing output practice

### Retention Risks
- Job demands displacing study time
- Impatience with slow progress in productive skills
- Career change eliminating the primary motivation

### Success Definition
- Writes professional emails without AI assistance
- Speaks in meetings for at least 2 minutes without switching to Spanish
- Scores B1+ in workplace English assessment
- Maintains daily practice regardless of work schedule

## P-04: Low-Confidence Speaking Learner (Aisha)

**Demographics:** 22 years old, Egypt, learning English  
**Background:** Excellent English education through school and university. Reads academic texts fluently (C1 receptive). Understands fast speech and media content. However, severe anxiety about speaking and making mistakes. Can write well (B2) but avoids speaking.

### Goals
- Reduce speaking anxiety
- Produce spoken English without excessive self-correction
- Build fluency over accuracy (temporarily)
- Achieve B2 speaking for academic and professional contexts

### Frustrations
- Apps that require speaking from day one trigger anxiety
- Peer comparison features increase self-consciousness
- Error correction in speaking feels like criticism
- Cannot find a safe, judgment-free speaking practice environment

### Skill Asymmetry
- Reading: B2/C1, Listening: B2, Speaking: A2, Writing: B2, Grammar: B2, Vocabulary: B2
- Massive gap between receptive and productive speaking skills (largest of all personas)

### Motivation
- **Primary**: Graduate studies abroad requiring oral proficiency exams
- **Secondary**: Social integration in English-speaking academic environment

### Accessibility Needs
- Optional speaking mode (can practice writing first)
- Private, no-audience practice environment
- Positive reinforcement focused (error correction balanced with encouragement)
- Self-paced: ability to rehearse before recording
- Text-to-speech models to hear correct pronunciation before producing

### Preferred Lesson Duration
- 20-25 minutes, including warm-up time to reduce anxiety

### Likely Failure Points
- Avoidance of speaking exercises entirely
- Excessive self-editing during writing tasks
- Abandoning speaking attempts after first correction
- Perfectionism preventing completion

### Retention Risks
- Anxiety spikes causing disengagement
- Comparing progress unfavorably to personas with less asymmetry
- Embarrassment from recorded audio (even if only self-reviewed)

### Success Definition
- Records speaking attempts without excessive retakes
- Participates in dialogue simulation for 3+ turns
- Self-reports reduced anxiety on post-session surveys
- Achieves B1+ speaking on diagnostic reassessment within 4 months
