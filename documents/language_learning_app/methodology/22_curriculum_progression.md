**Status:** Approved
**Version:** 1.0.0
**Last updated:** 2026-06-09

## Purpose

How the curriculum advances learners through CEFR levels A1 to C2, with spiral organization and per-skill progression.

## In scope

- Pedagogical framework definition and theoretical foundations
- Curriculum structure and progression design
- Assessment and competence model specifications
- Learning strategy and learner autonomy frameworks

## Out of scope

- Detailed lesson plans
- Specific curriculum item specifications
- Assessment rubric details
- Tutor training materials

## Core decisions

1. Methodology is grounded in established SLA research (Krashen, Swain, Long, Ellis, Vygotsky)
2. Task-Based Language Teaching is the core instructional approach
3. Personal narrative is the primary content source
4. CEFR 2020 is the reference framework including mediation and plurilingualism

## Acceptance criteria

1. Methodology is clearly articulated and internally consistent
2. Research foundations are cited appropriately
3. Methodology directly informs product design decisions
4. All recommendations are actionable for implementation

## CEFR level mapping

| CEFR | Label | Target learner | Approximate guided hours |
|------|-------|----------------|--------------------------|
| A1 | Breakthrough | Complete beginner | 90-100 |
| A2 | Waystage | Basic communicator | 180-200 |
| B1 | Threshold | Independent user | 350-400 |
| B2 | Vantage | Advanced independent | 500-600 |
| C1 | Effective operational | Proficient | 700-800 |
| C2 | Mastery | Near-native | 1000-1200 |

## Spiral curriculum design

Topics cycle at increasing complexity:

| Topic | A1 | A2 | B1 | B2 |
|-------|----|----|----|----|
| Self and family | Name, age, description | Personality, routine | Life story, relationships | Values, upbringing |
| Home and living | Rooms, furniture | Household tasks, neighborhood | Housing issues, renovation | Property, urban living |
| Work and study | Job title, workplace | Daily tasks, colleagues | Career history, projects | Industry trends |
| Food and eating | Basic foods, likes | Ordering, recipes | Cooking, dietary needs | Gastronomy, food culture |
| Travel | Basic directions | Transport, hotels | Itineraries, experiences | Travel writing |

## Micro-progression within levels

Each CEFR level is divided into 3 sub-levels: entering, developing, consolidating. A learner at 'B1 entering' is at the start of B1; at 'B1 consolidating' they are ready for B2 material.

## Per-skill progression

Reading, listening, speaking, writing, mediation, and interaction progress independently. Example profile:
- Reading: B1 consolidating
- Listening: B1 developing
- Speaking: A2 consolidating
- Writing: A2 entering
- Mediation: A2 developing

## Adaptive sequence

The curriculum engine selects the next item based on:
1. Prerequisite completion
2. Learner's weakest dimensions (prioritized)
3. Learner interest (preferred topics)
4. Spaced repetition needs
5. Variety (avoid same topic/mode repeatedly)
