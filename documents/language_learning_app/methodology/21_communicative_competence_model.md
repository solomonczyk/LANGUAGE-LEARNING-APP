# Communicative Competence Model

**Status:** Approved
**Version:** 1.0.0
**Last updated:** 2026-06-09

---

## Purpose

The CEFR-based model of communicative competence defining what learners need to develop and how the app addresses each competence area.

## In scope

- Pedagogical framework definition and theoretical foundations
- Curriculum structure and progression design
- Assessment and competence model specifications
- Learning strategy and learner autonomy frameworks

## Out of scope

- Detailed lesson plans
- Specific curriculum item specifications
- Assessment rubric details
- Tutor training materials

## Core decisions

1. Methodology is grounded in established SLA research (Krashen, Swain, Long, Ellis, Vygotsky)
2. Task-Based Language Teaching is the core instructional approach
3. Personal narrative is the primary content source
4. CEFR 2020 is the reference framework including mediation and plurilingualism

## Acceptance criteria

1. Methodology is clearly articulated and internally consistent
2. Research foundations are cited appropriately
3. Methodology directly informs product design decisions
4. All recommendations are actionable for implementation

## Competence components (CEFR 2020)

### Linguistic competence
Knowledge of vocabulary, grammar, syntax, phonology, orthography. Taught in context of communicative tasks, not in isolation.

**App features:** LKB-based grammar instruction, vocabulary tracking, pronunciation feedback, writing correction cycle.

### Sociolinguistic competence
Ability to use language appropriately in social contexts — politeness, register, dialect, cultural references.

**App features:** Pragmatics-focused lessons, register variation practice, cultural notes, situation-specific role plays.

### Pragmatic competence
Ability to use language to achieve communicative purposes — speech acts, implicature, discourse organization.

**App features:** Functional communication lessons, speech act practice (requesting, apologizing, refusing), discourse markers.

### Discourse competence
Ability to organize coherent and cohesive texts — narrative structure, topic management, reference maintenance.

**App features:** Narrative coherence feedback, retelling tasks, writing cycle with organization focus.

### Strategic competence
Ability to overcome communication problems — paraphrasing, clarification requests, repair strategies.

**App features:** Explicit strategy instruction, repair lessons, communication strategy practice.

### Plurilingual and pluricultural competence (CEFR 2018)
Ability to draw on multiple languages and cultural knowledge in communication.

**App features:** Cross-linguistic comparison, language transfer awareness, cultural mediation practice.

## Assessment per component

| Component | Assessment method | Evidence type |
|-----------|-------------------|---------------|
| Linguistic | Error analysis, quiz, writing | Correctness |
| Sociolinguistic | Role-play, situation response | Appropriateness |
| Pragmatic | Discourse completion, dialogue | Effectiveness |
| Discourse | Retelling, writing | Coherence/cohesion |
| Strategic | Problem-solving scenario | Strategy use |
| Plurilingual | Cross-linguistic task | Transfer awareness |
