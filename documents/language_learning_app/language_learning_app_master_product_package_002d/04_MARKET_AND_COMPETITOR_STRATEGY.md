# Market and Competitor Strategy

**Status:** CANONICAL  
**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Effective date:** 2026-06-10  
**Owner:** Product Owner  
**Change control:** Changes require a documented change request or ADR where specified.

## Competitive frame

The product competes for learner time and budget against:

- structured lesson apps;
- gamified practice apps;
- AI speaking tutors;
- private tutors;
- YouTube and free content;
- generic chatbots;
- self-study books and courses.

## Current competitor patterns

- Duolingo emphasizes adaptive, gamified lessons and AI-enabled speaking/roleplay.
- Babbel emphasizes short expert-designed lessons, conversation practice, and speech recognition.
- Busuu emphasizes structured courses, multiple skills, cultural content, and community feedback.
- Speak emphasizes speaking aloud, AI conversation, and instant feedback.

## Strategic gap to test

A possible gap exists between structured courses and open AI conversation: a system that uses the learner’s own stories and practical situations while preserving curriculum, validation, delayed recall, and deterministic progress authority.

This gap is a hypothesis and must be validated through interviews, completion data, retention, and payment—not assumed from competitor messaging.

## Competitive response strategy

Do not compete on:

- number of languages;
- content volume;
- streak intensity;
- mascot/gamification scale;
- unrestricted chat;
- cheapest subscription.

Compete on:

- active-skill recovery;
- personal relevance;
- visible evidence of independent output;
- safe, structured AI;
- practical communication transfer;
- guided 30-day recovery experience.

## Market evidence policy

Competitor pricing, adoption, and market-share claims are informational only unless dated and sourced. They must not be used as acceptance evidence for product decisions without current verification.

## Required ongoing market review

Review competitor positioning before:

- paid pilot;
- public MVP;
- major pricing change;
- major repositioning.

A market review may update messaging but cannot expand MVP scope automatically.
