# Commercial Model and Pricing Hypotheses

**Status:** CANONICAL  
**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Effective date:** 2026-06-10  
**Owner:** Product Owner  
**Change control:** Changes require a documented change request or ADR where specified.

## First commercial model

A limited 30-day paid pilot with guided onboarding and baseline/exit measurement.

## Offer hypothesis A — Guided pilot

- cohort: 10–20 learners;
- duration: 30 days;
- included: diagnostic, personalized plan, core lessons, review, progress summary, limited support;
- price test range: €19–€39;
- recommended first test: €29;
- no automatic renewal in first pilot;
- capacity-limited.

## Offer hypothesis B — Self-service subscription

Not tested until the guided pilot proves activation, retention, and manageable support.

Indicative future test:

- monthly: €9–€15;
- annual discount only after monthly willingness-to-pay evidence.

## Free boundary

Free may include:

- landing page;
- problem self-assessment;
- shortened diagnostic;
- one trial lesson;
- personalized preview.

Free must not include the entire 30-day recovery loop.

## Pricing experiment sequence

1. willingness-to-pay interviews;
2. reservation or waitlist intent;
3. paid pilot at €19;
4. paid pilot at €29;
5. paid pilot at €39 if demand and support justify it;
6. self-service monthly test.

Do not run multiple prices inside a very small cohort unless attribution remains clear.

## Commercial acceptance

A price hypothesis is not accepted because users say it is reasonable. Evidence requires actual payment or a meaningful deposit.

## Refund boundary

For the first pilot, document a simple, transparent refund rule before taking payment. Legal wording must be reviewed for the selling jurisdiction.

## Unit economics guardrails

Before public MVP:

- gross margin target after AI/infrastructure: at least 70%;
- support time target: below 30 minutes per active learner per week;
- AI cost target: below €2 per active learner per month for standard use;
- no paid acquisition scaling until retention and conversion are proven.

These are planning thresholds, not audited financial claims.
