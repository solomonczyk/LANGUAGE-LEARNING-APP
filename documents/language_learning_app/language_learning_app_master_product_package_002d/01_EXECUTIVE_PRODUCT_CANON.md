# Executive Product Canon

**Status:** CANONICAL  
**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Effective date:** 2026-06-10  
**Owner:** Product Owner  
**Change control:** Changes require a documented change request or ADR where specified.

## Product

A mobile language-learning system that restores active language ability through personal stories, real-life situations, visual retelling, audio retelling, guided correction, dialogue, delayed recall, and deterministic mastery evidence.

## Primary business objective

Reach a controlled paid pilot with real adult learners and obtain evidence of:

1. real recurring problem;
2. completed first-value journey;
3. measurable learning improvement;
4. week-one return;
5. willingness to pay;
6. sustainable support and AI cost.

## Primary launch segment

Adults aged approximately 25–50 who previously studied English to A2–B1/B2, now understand more than they can actively say, and need practical English for relocation, everyday life, work, or interviews.

## First sellable outcome

A 30-day guided recovery program helping a learner independently:

- tell a short story about a past event;
- explain a practical problem;
- describe a picture or situation;
- write a coherent short message;
- sustain a short guided dialogue.

This is a testable product hypothesis, not a guaranteed marketing claim.

## First commercial form

A limited paid pilot with onboarding support, measurable baseline and exit assessment, and controlled cohort size.

## MVP decision boundary

The MVP exists to prove the full learner loop and paid demand. It is not intended to provide all lesson modes, all languages, teacher marketplace, social network, public leaderboard, or production-scale automation.

## Decision authority

- Product Owner: scope, segment, pricing experiments, stage decisions.
- Technical Lead: architecture and release readiness.
- Learning/Methodology reviewer: learning validity.
- Operator reviewer: visual and user-flow acceptance.
- Security gate: zero-tolerance controls.

## Final project states

`GO`, `HOLD`, `PIVOT`, or `STOP`.

A stage cannot silently roll into the next stage.
