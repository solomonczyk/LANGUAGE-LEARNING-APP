# Primary Customer, Jobs, and Buying Context

**Status:** CANONICAL  
**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Effective date:** 2026-06-10  
**Owner:** Product Owner  
**Change control:** Changes require a documented change request or ADR where specified.

## Primary persona

**Returning active-skill learner**

Profile:

- adult learner;
- prior English exposure at A2–B1/B2;
- long break or little speaking practice;
- receptive skills stronger than productive skills;
- fear of errors or embarrassment;
- needs practical output, not abstract grammar study.

## Core jobs to be done

### Functional jobs

- explain what happened;
- describe a problem;
- respond in a short conversation;
- write a clear message;
- recover forgotten vocabulary and constructions;
- prepare for relocation, work, or interview situations.

### Emotional jobs

- stop feeling “I understand but cannot speak”;
- reduce fear of mistakes;
- see visible evidence of progress;
- regain control over learning.

### Social jobs

- communicate without depending on a translator;
- appear competent in everyday or work situations;
- participate in conversations instead of avoiding them.

## Main pains

- generic phrases unrelated to personal life;
- free chat without progression;
- correction that rewrites everything;
- streak pressure without real skill growth;
- single-level labels that hide skill asymmetry;
- speaking exercises that feel artificial.

## Purchase triggers

- relocation or job search;
- interview deadline;
- repeated communication failure;
- return to study after a long break;
- dissatisfaction with passive app usage;
- need for structured speaking practice.

## Reasons not to buy

- unclear outcome;
- too much time per day;
- no confidence in AI feedback;
- lack of privacy clarity;
- price higher than perceived personal benefit;
- experience resembles a generic chatbot;
- onboarding feels like a test rather than help.

## Initial geography and language support

Primary discovery may target Ukrainian- and Russian-speaking adults living in Europe or preparing for international work. Interface/support language should not be interpreted as a political identity claim.

## Excluded primary audiences for first launch

- young children;
- schools and classroom administration;
- exam-only learners;
- advanced academic writing users;
- users seeking live tutors;
- enterprise procurement.
