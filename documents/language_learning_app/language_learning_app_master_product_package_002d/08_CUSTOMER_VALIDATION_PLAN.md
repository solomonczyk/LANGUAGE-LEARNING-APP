# Customer Validation Plan

**Status:** CANONICAL  
**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Effective date:** 2026-06-10  
**Owner:** Product Owner  
**Change control:** Changes require a documented change request or ADR where specified.

## Validation sequence

### V0 — Problem interviews

Target: 12–15 interviews.

Pass evidence:

- at least 8 participants describe the active-output problem without prompting;
- at least 6 have tried an alternative;
- at least 5 describe a recent costly or frustrating communication failure;
- at least 4 agree to test a solution.

### V1 — Message and landing validation

Target: 100 qualified visits or 30 direct outreach conversations.

Pass evidence:

- ≥20% diagnostic-start rate from landing;
- or ≥25% positive response from highly targeted outreach;
- at least 10 qualified waitlist signups.

### V2 — Concierge lesson test

Target: 8–10 learners.

Pass evidence:

- ≥70% complete the lesson;
- ≥60% report the lesson used personally relevant material;
- ≥50% request another lesson;
- no unresolved critical safety issue.

### V3 — Controlled product alpha

Target: 10–20 learners.

Pass evidence:

- ≥70% first-value completion;
- ≥40% Day-7 return;
- median time to first value ≤20 minutes;
- critical flow failure <5%;
- zero cross-user data incidents.

### V4 — Paid pilot

Target: at least 10 paying learners.

Pass evidence:

- at least 10 real payments;
- ≥80% activation;
- ≥50% week-one return;
- ≥35% 30-day completion;
- support workload below guardrail;
- no zero-tolerance safety event.

## Interview policy

Do not ask “Would you use this?” as the primary question. Ask about past behaviour, recent failures, alternatives, time spent, money spent, and concrete deadlines.

## Learning outcome evidence

Collect:

- baseline independent output;
- end-of-pilot independent output;
- recurring-error rate;
- delayed recall;
- transfer task;
- learner confidence;
- human reviewer sample.

## Invalid evidence

- likes;
- compliments;
- survey intention without action;
- unqualified traffic;
- synthetic users;
- operator-created completion;
- technical success without learner completion.
