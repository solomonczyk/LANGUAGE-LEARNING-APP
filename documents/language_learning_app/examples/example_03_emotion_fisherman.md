## Visual scenario

An image of an older man sitting on a dock by a lake, holding a fishing rod. His face shows concentration mixed with anticipation. The float on the water is moving — there's a bite. The scene is calm: morning light, mist on the water, trees in the background.

The task does NOT have a single "correct" emotional interpretation. The learner is assessed on quality of justification.

## Task 1: Describe the scene

**System:** Describe what you see in the picture.

**Learner:** There is an old man sitting on a dock. He has a fishing rod. It is morning. The lake is calm.

**System:** Good. Now, how does he feel?

## Task 2: Emotion identification

**System:** What emotion do you see on his face? Why do you think that?

**Learner:** He looks... concentrated. Maybe excited. Because the float moves.

**System:** Good observation! Let's work on the word: he is **concentrating**. Try: "He looks like he is concentrating because..."

**Learner:** He looks like he is concentrating because the fish is biting.

## Task 3: Perspective-taking

**System:** Imagine I have never been fishing. Explain to me why he feels this way.

**Learner:** When you go fishing, you wait for a long time. Sometimes nothing happens. But now something is happening. The float is moving. That means a fish is interested. He is excited because maybe he will catch a fish.

**System:** Excellent explanation! You made it very clear.

## Task 4: Alternative perspective

**System:** What if I told you that this man has been fishing every morning for 40 years? Does that change how you see his emotion? Maybe he feels something different.

**Learner:** Maybe he is not excited. Maybe he is... calm. He does this every day. He knows what to do. He is patient.

**System:** Great! Now you're seeing two different interpretations. Both are valid.

## Assessment

The learner is assessed on:
- Quality of emotional vocabulary (concentrated, excited, patient) ✓
- Quality of justification (because the float moves, because he has been doing this for 40 years) ✓
- Ability to take another perspective ✓
- Grammatical accuracy (minor issues with articles and prepositions)
- Discourse markers (when, that means, maybe)

**No score for "correct" emotional reading** — multiple interpretations accepted.
