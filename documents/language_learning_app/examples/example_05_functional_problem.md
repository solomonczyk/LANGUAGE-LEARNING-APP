## Scenario

The learner needs to call customer service to explain that a delivery is late. They ordered a laptop for work, and it was supposed to arrive 3 days ago. They need it for an important project.

## Phase 1: Preparation

**System:** You need to call customer service about a late delivery. Let's prepare.

Key information:
- Order #: LAP-789-XZ
- Ordered: 10 days ago
- Promised delivery: 3 days ago
- Item: Laptop for work project
- Project deadline: 2 days from now

What phrases might you need?

**Learner:** I want to check my delivery. My order number is LAP-789-XZ.

**System:** Good start. Let's add some polite phrases: "I'd like to check..." "Could you please..."

## Phase 2: Role-play

**System (as customer service):** "Hello, customer service. How can I help you?"

**Learner:** Hello. I'd like to check my delivery. My order number is LAP-789-XZ.

**System (CS):** "Let me check... I see your order was dispatched 5 days ago. Is there a problem?"

**Learner:** Yes, it was supposed to arrive 3 days ago but it didn't. I need this laptop for my work. I have a project deadline in 2 days.

**System (CS):** "I apologize for the delay. Let me look into this for you."

**Learner:** Could you please expedite the delivery? Or maybe I can pick it up from the warehouse?

## Phase 3: Feedback

**System:** Let's review your language:

1. "I'd like to check" — perfect, polite ✅
2. "It was supposed to arrive..." — good use of passive ✅
3. "Could you please expedite..." — excellent polite request ✅
4. "Maybe I can pick it up" — good alternative suggestion ✅

One improvement: "I have a project deadline in 2 days" could be more formal: "I have a project deadline in 2 days, so this delivery is quite urgent."

## Phase 4: Transfer

**System:** Now imagine you ordered food for a party and it arrived 2 hours late and cold. Call the restaurant to explain. Use what you learned.

**Learner:** Hello, I'd like to speak about my delivery. My order was for 6 PM but it arrived at 8 PM. The food was cold. Could you please... refund?

**System:** Excellent transfer! You used the polite request form correctly. Try: "Could you please issue a refund?" instead of just "refund."

**Learner:** Could you please issue a refund?
