**Status:** Approved

## Purpose

This document defines the high-level product vision for a personalized language learning mobile application. The app teaches users to express their own thoughts and describe their world — not repeat textbook phrases.

## In scope

- Core product philosophy and vision
- Communicative-competence-driven approach
- AI-powered adaptive learning with deterministic safeguards
- The product promise: what learners will be able to do

## Out of scope

- Implementation details
- UI/UX mockups
- Technical architecture specifications
- Market analysis

## Core decisions

1. The app teaches users to describe their own life, not repeat textbook phrases
2. Communicative goals drive lessons, not grammar topics
3. AI is a constrained assistant, not an autonomous teacher
4. Personal meaning is the primary motivational driver
5. The app serves as a bridge to real-world communication

## Acceptance criteria

1. Vision is clearly articulated and unambiguous
2. Core philosophy is distinguishable from competitors
3. All product decisions traceable to this vision
4. Stakeholders agree on the stated direction

## The vision

Language learning today is broken. Learners repeat decontextualized phrases, memorize vocabulary lists, and fill in grammar gaps — yet when faced with a real conversation, they freeze.

**Our vision is a language learning app that teaches you to express YOUR life in another language.**

Instead of repeating someone else's sentences about someone else's world, every lesson starts with what matters to you: your morning routine, your cat's mischief, your frustration with public transport, your opinion about a movie, your memory of a childhood event, your plans for the weekend.

## Core approach

The learner tells a story or responds to a scenario. The AI analyzes their language — grammar, vocabulary, coherence, pragmatic appropriateness. It identifies areas for improvement. It scaffolds practice around those areas using the learner's own content. It provides corrective feedback that respects the learner's voice while guiding toward target-like norms. It schedules review of weak areas and celebrates progress.

Every lesson has a clear communicative goal. Not "learn the past tense" but "describe something that happened to you yesterday." Grammar serves communication, not the reverse.

## Product principles

1. **Personal meaning first.** Content that matters to the learner is more memorable, more motivating, and more likely to transfer to real use.
2. **Communication over grammar.** Language exists for communication. Grammar is a tool, not the goal.
3. **Authentic tasks.** Lessons prepare learners for real situations they will actually face.
4. **Adaptive scaffolding.** Support is calibrated to the learner's current ability for each specific skill and construction.
5. **Learner agency.** The learner chooses topics, sets goals, and controls pace.
6. **Privacy by design.** Personal stories stay private. Data is protected.

## The product promise

By completing the curriculum, learners will be able to:

- Describe their life, work, and interests in the target language
- Handle everyday situations (ordering, asking for help, making arrangements)
- Narrate past experiences and future plans
- Express opinions, emotions, and reactions
- Mediate between speakers of different languages
- Repair communication breakdowns
- Continue learning independently

This is not a promise of fluency in 30 days. It is a promise of meaningful, usable, personal language ability built through consistent practice.

## Lesson sources

The system supports 14 lesson modes, each providing a different channel for language input and production:

1. Personal narrative — learner tells their own story
2. Suggested situation — realistic scenario provided by the system
3. Visual single scene — one image as the stimulus
4. Visual sequence — multiple images forming a story
5. Illustrated emotion and perspective — interpreting feelings from images
6. Audio narrative — short audio monologue
7. Audio dialogue — recorded conversation
8. Reading-based — text as primary input
9. Functional communication — real-world task (ordering, booking)
10. Repair lesson — focused error correction
11. Mediation lesson — relaying information between parties
12. Review lesson — spaced repetition consolidation
13. Progress checkpoint — milestone assessment
14. Assessment lesson — formal evaluation
