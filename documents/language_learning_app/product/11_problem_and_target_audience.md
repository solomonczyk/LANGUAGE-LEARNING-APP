# Problem and Target Audience — Language Learning App

**Status:** Approved  
**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Last updated:** 2026-06-09

## Purpose

Analysis of the core problems with current language learning approaches and definition of the target audience for the personalized language learning app.

## In scope

- Identifying gaps in existing language learning solutions
- Defining primary and secondary target audiences
- Understanding learner pain points and motivations
- Mapping user needs to product features

## Out of scope

- General education market analysis (covered in market document)
- Corporate training market analysis
- Children's language learning
- Examination preparation market

## Core decisions

1. Current apps teach language as a system, not as communication
2. Learners want to talk about THEIR lives, not generic topics
3. Adult learners have specific, practical communication needs
4. The gap between app learning and real communication is the key problem to solve

## Acceptance criteria

1. Problem statement is validated by target audience research
2. Target audience is clearly defined with demographics
3. Pain points are addressable by the proposed solution
4. Audience segments prioritized for MVP

## The problems

### Problem 1: Decontextualized learning

Learners spend months on vocabulary and grammar that never appears in their actual communication needs. The language learned does not transfer to real situations. A learner can say "the cat is on the table" in 15 languages but cannot explain to a landlord that the heating is broken.

### Problem 2: One-size-fits-all

Every learner follows the same path regardless of their L1, existing knowledge, learning goals, or interests. A Brazilian learning English for business travel follows the same curriculum as a German learning English for academic reading.

### Problem 3: No personal voice

Learners produce sentences that are technically correct but feel hollow — they don't express the learner's personality, humor, opinions, or perspective. Language without personal voice is not truly communicative.

### Problem 4: Passive over active

Most apps focus on recognition (multiple choice, matching) rather than production. Learners can recognize thousands of words but cannot produce a coherent paragraph about their own life.

### Problem 5: No real communication practice

Apps provide drills, not conversations. Learners never practice initiating, maintaining, and repairing real communication. When faced with a real interaction, they freeze.

### Problem 6: Motivation collapse

Without personal relevance, motivation fades after the initial novelty. Gamification provides short-term engagement but not the deep motivation that comes from meaningful communication.

## Target audience

### Primary: Adult learners (22–45)

**Demographics:** Urban, educated, employed or studying, digitally native, globally mobile.

**Context:** Have tried at least one language app before. Found it inadequate for real communication. Motivated by specific life needs:

- **Expats and immigrants** — need to function in daily life. Target level: A2–B1. Pain point: can survive but cannot truly express themselves.
- **Professionals** — need language for work meetings, emails, networking. Target level: B1–B2. Pain point: technical vocabulary is fine, but social and persuasive language is weak.
- **Frequent travelers** — want deeper cultural connection, not just tourist phrases. Target level: A1–A2. Pain point: phrasebooks fail in unexpected situations.
- **Heritage learners** — want to reclaim a family language with personal connection. Target level: varies. Pain point: understand family but cannot respond.

**Psychographics:** Value authenticity, personalization, and efficiency. Willing to invest time if outcomes are real. Frustrated with gamification that feels childish. Want respect for their adult intelligence.

### Secondary: Lifelong learners (45+)

Learning for cognitive stimulation, travel, or personal growth. Need gentle onboarding, clear progress signals, and respect for their experience and life wisdom.

### Tertiary: Advanced learners (B2+)

Struggling to reach C1. Need sophisticated feedback on pragmatic nuance, register, and discourse-level coherence. Frustrated by apps that only offer beginner content.

## User needs mapping

| Need | Current solution gap | Our approach |
|------|---------------------|--------------|
| Talk about my life | Apps teach generic content | Personal narrative as core |
| Real communication | Drills, not conversations | Communicative tasks |
| Appropriate level | One-level-fits-all | Multidimensional profile |
| My voice preserved | Correct everything | Preserve style, correct errors |
| Flexible practice | Rigid path | Adaptive selection |
| See real progress | Time-based metrics | Evidence-based mastery |
